The form found above this text aims to calculate if there is a trend in the time series. A trend indicates the long-term change in the variable of a time series. All statistical trend functions calculate how likely it is that the trend is present due to some random process. This likelihood is expressed as a percentage called the p-value. For example, if the p-value is 10%, then there is a 10% chance that the trend observed in the time series is due to randomness Normally in statistical test we define two hypotheses. Hypotheses 0 is that there is no trend in the time series while Hypotheses 1 is that there is trend in the time-series.

Making time-series data stationary is a crucial preprocessing step in time-series analysis. Stationarity implies that statistical properties such as mean, variance, and autocorrelation remain constant over time. By achieving stationarity, we can simplify the modeling process and enable more accurate forecasting and analysis. Stationary data allows us to apply a wider range of statistical techniques, assumptions, and models, ultimately leading to better insights and predictions from the data. Detrending by differencing is a common method used to achieve stationarity in time-series data. By taking the difference between consecutive observations, we remove the trend component from the data, making it stationary. Trends in time-series data represent long-term changes or patterns that may obscure underlying patterns or relationships.
